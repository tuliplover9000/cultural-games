Ô Ăn Quan

Vietnam — Board

How to Play ▼

Ô Ăn Quan is a two-player mancala-style game played on a board with 10 small pits and 2 large "quan" pits, one on each end.

  1. Each small pit starts with 5 seeds. Players take turns — Player 1 controls the bottom row, Player 2 the top.
  2. Click any of your pits to pick up all seeds and distribute them one by one counterclockwise.
  3. Continue sowing: if the last seed lands in a non-empty pit, pick up those seeds and keep going.
  4. Capture: if the last seed lands in an empty pit and the pit next to it has seeds, capture those seeds into your quan.
  5. The game ends when all pits on one player's side are empty. The player with the most seeds in their quan wins.
Cultural Context ▼

Ô Ăn Quan — literally "mandarin's squares" — is one of Vietnam's most beloved traditional children's games, played across the country for centuries. The game is typically drawn directly on the ground or on a flat stone, using pebbles or seeds as pieces.

The game develops strategic thinking, counting skills, and spatial reasoning, which is why it has long been used as both entertainment and informal education for children. It belongs to the global mancala family of games, found across Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Middle East — a testament to shared human ingenuity across cultures.
