Tiến Lên

Vietnam — Card

How to Play ▼

Tiến Lên ("go forward") is a 4-player Vietnamese shedding card game. The goal is to be the first player to play all your cards.

  1. Each player is dealt 13 cards. Card ranking: 3 (lowest) → 4 → 5 → … → K → A → 2 (highest). Suits rank: Spades < Clubs < Diamonds < Hearts.
  2. The player with the 3 of Spades goes first and must include it in their opening play.
  3. Players take turns playing a hand that beats the previous one — matching the same hand type (single, pair, triple, sequence, etc.) but with higher value.
  4. If you cannot or choose not to beat the current hand, pass. Once all other players pass, the last player to play leads the next round with any hand.
  5. Special rule: a 2 can only be beaten by four-of-a-kind or a sequence of 3+ pairs.
  6. First player to empty their hand wins.
Cultural Context ▼

Tiến Lên is arguably Vietnam's most popular card game, played in homes, coffee shops, and community centers across the country. Its name, meaning "go forward," captures both the game mechanic and a broader Vietnamese cultural value of persistence and advancement.

The game is part of the larger family of shedding card games found across East and Southeast Asia, including the Chinese game Zheng Shangyou. Vietnamese communities worldwide have carried Tiến Lên with them, making it a touchstone of cultural identity far from home. A gathering of Vietnamese friends is rarely complete without a few rounds.
