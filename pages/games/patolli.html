Patolli

Aztec Mesoamerica — Dice

How to Play ▼

Patolli is an ancient race game played on a cross-shaped board with 52 spaces. Each player races 6 pieces around the full circuit.

  1. Bean Dice: throw 5 marked beans. Count how many land marked-side up — that's your move distance. All 5 unmarked = move 10. All 5 marked = move 5 AND take another turn.
  2. After rolling, choose which of your pieces to move. Only pieces with legal moves are selectable.
  3. Capture: landing on an opponent's piece sends it back to start. Safe zones protect pieces — they cannot be captured there.
  4. You cannot move a piece to a space your own piece already occupies.
  5. Race all 6 of your pieces around the full circuit and off the board to win.
  6. A virtual tribute (coin) system tracks captures and earnings throughout the game.
Cultural Context ▼

Patolli is one of the oldest games in the Americas, played by the Aztec (Mexica) people and their predecessors for at least 2,000 years. Spanish conquistadors documented it in the 16th century with astonishment — noting that Aztec nobles and commoners alike played it with intense enthusiasm, wagering precious stones, blankets, and even their own freedom.

The board's cross shape carries deep cosmological significance: the four arms represent the four cardinal directions and the four seasons, aligning the game with the Aztec 52-year calendar cycle — the same number as spaces on the board. The game was also associated with the god Macuilxochitl, patron of games and pleasure. Spanish colonizers eventually banned Patolli as pagan, nearly erasing it from history. Digital preservation efforts like this one help ensure it is not forgotten.
