How to Play Bầu Cua Tôm Cá (Vietnamese Dice Game)

Bầu Cua Tôm Cá - meaning Gourd, Crab, Rooster, Fish - is Vietnam's most beloved festival dice game, played at Tết (Lunar New Year) celebrations across the country and diaspora. Bet on symbols, shake three dice, and win if your symbol appears. Simple, fast, and irresistibly fun.

Note: This game uses virtual coins only. No real money is involved.

The Six Symbols

Each face of the three dice shows one of six traditional symbols:

Setting Up

  1. One player acts as the "house" - they shake the dice and pay out winnings.
  2. All other players are bettors. Each bettor starts with a set number of virtual coins.
  3. Bettors choose one or more symbols to bet on and place their wager.

How to Play

  1. All bettors place their bets on one or more of the six symbols.
  2. The house shakes a cup containing three dice and reveals the result.
  3. If a symbol you bet on appears on one die, you win 1× your bet back (plus your stake).
  4. If your symbol appears on two dice, you win 2× your bet.
  5. If your symbol appears on all three dice, you win 3× your bet.
  6. If your symbol does not appear, you lose your stake.

You can bet on multiple symbols at once - each bet is evaluated independently.

Cultural Context

Bầu Cua Tôm Cá is inseparable from Tết Nguyên Đán (Vietnamese Lunar New Year). During the festival, families and communities gather around a mat or low table spread with the six symbols, placing bets with paper money or tokens while the house - often the eldest at the table - shakes the dice inside a bowl.

The game is also a street game: during Tết, vendors set up stalls at markets and festivals, drawing in players with the rhythmic sound of dice rattling in a ceramic cup. The six symbols - gourd, crab, rooster, fish, prawn, and deer - are taken from traditional Vietnamese iconography, each carrying its own folklore associations with luck and prosperity.

For Vietnamese diaspora communities around the world, playing Bầu Cua at Tết is a way to reconnect with home - the sounds, the gathering, and the friendly competition carry the warmth of the holiday across generations and continents.
